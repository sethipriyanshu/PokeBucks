Financial Tips
Start Learning
50/30/20 Rule

Try allocating 50% of your income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment.

Emergency Fund

Aim to save 3-6 months of essential expenses in an easily accessible account for emergencies.

Automate Your Savings

Set up automatic transfers to your savings account on payday to make saving effortless.

24-Hour Rule

Wait 24 hours before making non-essential purchases over $50 to curb impulse spending.

Track Your Spending

Use apps or spreadsheets to track your expenses and identify areas where you can cut back.

Review Your Subscriptions

Regularly review subscriptions and cancel those you no longer use to save money.

Start Early

The earlier you start investing, the more you can take advantage of compound interest.

Diversify

Don’t put all your money in one investment; diversify across stocks, bonds, and other assets.

Educate Yourself

Take time to understand the basics of investing before you jump in.
